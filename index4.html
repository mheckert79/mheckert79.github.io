CASTLEVANIA

1/12/2006

While being nostalgic about the Playstation earlier, I decided to sit down and pop in Castlevania: Symphony of the Night, one of my all-time favorite games. Holy shit, it’s just as good, if not better than I remember. 1[] The game begins with you fully decked-out with all your powers and abilities so you can appreciate when they are almost instantly ripped away from you to start your journey. Not to mention the kickass soundtrack that accompanies the entire experience, with Lost Painting and Dracula’s Castle being standouts for me. [music] Although the flipped castle seems a bit tedious after such an excellent first half, the whole game front to back is a marvel. Although it really can’t be compared to the classic Castlevania games, SOTN is definitely one of the best in the series. -Charlie.

RAMBLINGS

1/24/2006

Ever since hanging out with Mike and Sam a few days ago I’ve been feeling so weird about the future. I’m almost upset that I like them so much and that we can’t just have the same friendship we had freshman year forever. -Charlie.

FRIENDS AGAIN

1/21/2006

Me, Mike, and Sam hung out all together for the first time in what seemed like forever. Mike has been so busy fighting with or avoiding his parents I feel like we never see him anymore, so it was great to kick back in the basement, play GTA, and listen to music for a few hours on a friday night. I think we’ve all been feeling a little disconnected from each other now that we’re in our last semester in high school, and Sam is for sure moving to the west coast for some biology degree. It was just nice to be together again in one place.


-Charlie.
