BENDING THE WORLD AROUND ME

12/07/2005

One thing I forgot to mention in my other databending posts was how I use Audacity to bend my files and how really any format, not only pictures, can be databent. One example of this is that when I convert an image into . TIFF format, I can then import that raw data to Audacity, an free audio editing software. Because Audacity interprets the numerical data that composes the picture as audio, you get a waveform that you can listen to! Below is the databent image, as well as what it sounds like.







I’ve also included the audio file of my site header as another example.




This also got me pontificating about extrapolating this idea to real life. I’ve always loved the sci-fi concept of uploading everyone’s consciousness to the same server, creating a perfect recreation of our own life. Would it still be “real”? Would this perfect recreation be real if life on earth ceased to exist elsewhere?

I know this is kind of a lot, but databending always gets me thinking this way. What if we could reduce everything to more simpler terms to reconstruct them again in a better, or more interesting way?

ABOUT DATABENDING

11/18/05

As I mentioned before, I’m pretty into the art of databending. For those unfamiliar with the concept, it’s taking the raw data of anything (usually an image, but only certain formats work) and reconfiguring it into something new. Like I said, I love that it reminds us that everything we experience on the web can be reduced to data—just numbers on a screen with no meaning unless put together in the right way.

One of the more common, and my personal favorite utilization of this, is using databending to create glitched-out freaky edits of pictures found online. As you could probably tell, I love using screenshots from my favorite anime series, like Neon Genesis Evangelion and Serial Experiments Lain.



-Charlie.
